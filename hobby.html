Too Many Interests, So Let's Start with Dance

Since I have way too many interests, I'll just introduce a few of them. From the photos and my self-introduction, you've probably already noticed that I really love dancing. More broadly speaking, I love art. But I also know that if I became an artist, I'd probably starve to death, so I ended up studying Foreign Languages and Literature instead.

Let's start with dancing. I've wanted to learn how to dance for years. It all started back in kindergarten. After my dad played Step Up 3D, he asked me a question: "Do you want to learn how to dance?" And that was the moment the seed was planted.

Little me looked at Moose in the movie and thought he was the coolest person alive. "Yes!" I answered immediately. To which my dad simply replied: "But there's nowhere around here for you to learn." And that was the end of that.

I think that was the moment I developed my hip-hop spirit. Then why did you even ask me? That was probably the only thought six-year-old me had at the time. Pretty ahead of its time, if you ask me.

After I entered elementary school, there was a street dance class offered on Fridays as an after-school activity. I ran home excitedly to tell my parents about it. The response I got was: "Then who's going to pick you up?" But I didn't give up.

When I got to middle school, we finally had club activities, and I immediately wanted to join the dance club. Unfortunately, my homeroom teacher had some stereotypes about street dance. After telling us that we could choose any club except the dance club, I once again missed the chance to learn dancing. So I ended up joining the guitar club instead. But that's another story. For the sake of keeping this page from turning into a novel, I'll save that one for another time.

Then came high school. I was part of the first generation under Taiwan's new 108 Curriculum Guidelines. Because of university admission considerations, I chose to attend a private high school and was forced into an academic club: the Chemistry Research Club. Unexpectedly, that decision connected me with National Tsing Hua University long before I ever became a student there. The connection was the Tsing Hua Cup Chemistry Competition.

I still remember that because of the pandemic, the competition was held online. Feeling extremely confident, I finished my exam, calculated the timing carefully, and submitted it. Why am I emphasizing the timing? Because submitting early would automatically give you a zero.

Life, however, is unpredictable. Whether it was because the system clock wasn't synchronized with my computer clock or for some other mysterious reason, two weeks after the competition ended, I discovered that I was the only participant in my club who got a zero. For at least two weeks, I didn't dare face my chemistry teacher.

You're probably wondering what any of this has to do with dancing. Nothing. It has absolutely nothing to do with dancing. I just wanted to share it.

Then I entered university. During my freshman year, an upperclassman lured me into joining the guitar club again. Coming from the countryside, I had no idea that university students could join more than one club at the same time. As a result, my foolish self missed the dance club once again.

When I became a sophomore, even though I somehow got lured into becoming a club officer as well, I had already made up my mind. I was determined, two million percent determined, that I had to join the dance club at least once in my life. So I finally did it. I joined the dance club without hesitation. And that's how my journey as a dancer began.
