Short Autobiography

Learning across language, data, and movement.

Home Hobby

Eason Tsai

Hi, I'm Cheng-Ting Tsai. What a painfully cliche way to start. My friends all call me Eason Tsai. I'm currently studying Foreign Languages and Literature, with a double major in Quantitative Finance. Just in case I ever forget, I overloaded my credits for two semesters in a row during the 2025 academic year. I was basically dying. Don't do that in the future.

Now, a proper introduction. I'm from Taichung, or as I like to call it, Taichill City. In theory, it's probably the last place where people should be rushing through life. But because I was feeling lost, I ended up forcing myself to finish my credits as quickly as possible so I could graduate early. Looking back, I regret it a lot. It was completely against my nature.

You can probably tell that I'm the kind of person who likes to move at a slower pace. I need a little room to breathe in everything I do. And just in case this self-introduction page somehow gets picked by the professor as a model website, I'd like to take this opportunity to tell any future students who happen to take this course: please don't overload your credits for no reason.

Everyone lives in a different time zone. It's really okay to move a little slower. Don't let the people around you scare you into forcing yourself down a path you don't actually want. This is university. It might very well be the last safe harbor you'll have in your life. So staying here for a while, taking things in, and actually experiencing life isn't a bad thing at all.

Most importantly, if there's something you want to do, go do it. Don't leave regrets behind during the years when you're most allowed to make mistakes. As long as it won't get you killed, go give it a try.

I'm a man with way too many interests, which is also why I constantly struggle with making choices, especially when it comes to life decisions. Luckily, I'm not that introverted. I've been fortunate enough to have a lot of wonderful friends to talk to, exchange ideas with, and occasionally figure life out together.

When I have free time, I like making coffee, listening to music, and dancing. To me, the most important thing in life is to truly experience it and do my best to live it well.

If you've actually made it all the way to the end of this autobiography, I'm honored that I got three minutes of your time. I also hope you were able to take something away from it, because I genuinely enjoy sharing things with people. And to you, whoever ended up crossing paths with me because of these not-so-nutritious words, I hope your future is just as colorful and full of stories worth sharing.
