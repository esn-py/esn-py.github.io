Expository Essay Writing

A reading guide and article analysis based on One of the Loneliest Countries Finds Companionship in Dogs.

What Is an Expository Essay?

An expository essay explains a topic instead of simply arguing from personal feeling. A strong essay gives readers a clear main idea, connects sentences smoothly, arranges paragraphs in a logical order, and ends with a useful takeaway.

This page uses the New York Times article about dogs in South Korea to show how those four elements work in an actual piece of writing. The article explains why dogs have become important companions in a country with a very low birthrate and many people living alone.

Four Elements

Thesis statement tells readers the main focus.

Cohesive writing connects nearby sentences.

Coherent writing connects paragraphs and subtopics.

Takeaway leaves readers with a final point.

1. Thesis Statement

Tip A thesis statement should appear near the beginning and tell readers what the whole article will explain. It does not need to be argumentative, but it should be clear enough to guide the rest of the writing.

Article example The opening summary says that dogs have become pampered family members in South Korea, where the birthrate is extremely low and many people live alone.

Analysis

This sentence works as the thesis because it connects the article title, the national context, and the emotional subject. The article is not only about cute pets. It is about how social change makes dogs fill a family-like role for many people.

2. Cohesive Writing

Tip Cohesion is about sentence-to-sentence connection. Writers can repeat key words, use pronouns, contrast ideas, or add details that clearly grow from the previous sentence.

Article example The article first describes an emotional funeral scene. The next sentence reveals that the ceremony was for Dalkong, a white poodle.

Analysis

The first sentence makes readers expect a human funeral. The next sentence changes that expectation, but it still connects smoothly because words such as funeral objects, companion, and cremation prepare readers for grief. This controlled reveal creates cohesion and also makes the topic memorable.

3. Coherent Writing

Tip Coherence is about paragraph-to-paragraph movement. A coherent essay does not jump randomly. Each new paragraph should answer a question created by the previous one.

Article example After the funeral scene, the article moves to South Korea's pet-care industry, household changes, low birthrate, and legal debate about animal status.

Analysis

The article begins with one woman and one dog, then slowly widens the focus to neighborhoods, businesses, families, and national policy. This order is coherent because every wider topic explains why the opening scene matters beyond one personal story.

4. Takeaway

Tip A takeaway should help readers understand why the article matters. It usually returns to the main idea while adding a final reflection.

Article example Near the end, the article returns to the question of whether dogs should be treated more like family members when they die.

Analysis

The takeaway is not simply that many South Koreans like dogs. The stronger final idea is that companionship can change social habits, business practices, and even legal language. The article therefore completes a writing cycle: it starts with a dog treated like family and ends by asking what that family-like status should mean.
